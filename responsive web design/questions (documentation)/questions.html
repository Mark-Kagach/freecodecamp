Introduction

"To ask the right question is already half the solution of a problem." -- Carl Jung

I find this to be remarkably true. Great questions force you to think in a new, more powerful way. They frequently do so by showing what to focus on. One example to show my point.

What is your bottleneck? The idea of working ONLY on your bottleneck is probably the most powerful productivity idea I've met. It is so powerful that sometimes I wish I didn't understand it, as now doing anything but the bottleneck is ruined for me. It also fits perfectly with the common advice you encounter when studying great people -- focus. Now you always know what to focus on.

So questions seem to follow a power law: a handful few give outsized returns. Thus, as someone who likes to engage in life-long projects it seems only obvious to start my own collection of "exponential" questions.

If you have a great question or think some question is erroneous please write me at mark@markkagach.com I generously reward those that fix my errors.

The Universe and Humanity

  • What is the nature of the universe?
  • How do we create knowledge?
  • What is the main bottleneck hindering humanity's progress?
  • What are the key drivers of humanity's progress?
  • How to measure humanity's progress?
  • On what metrics should society be evaluated?
  • In helping others, should one focus on raising the ceiling, the floor or something in the middle?
  • How to raise humanity's ambition?
  • What are the big mistakes humanity is doing that already have solutions?
  • What are the incentives?

Personal

  • What are your goals in life?
  • What problems you choose to dedicate your life to?
  • What is your bottleneck?
  • What is something ONLY you can do?
  • What is something that you have natural talent and passion for at the same time?
  • What is your competitive edge?
  • What is your process for correcting your errors?
  • What are your most important beliefs, and how much time you spend proving them wrong?
  • What were your favorite ideas that actually turned out to be wrong?
  • What important truth do very few people agree with you on?
  • Do you understand the idea your opponent defends better than they do?
  • How can you achieve your 10-year plan in the next 6 months?
  • What is your circle of competence?
  • What are your strengths that you have double downed on?
  • What are your weaknesses and how do you compensate them?
  • What are the simple ideas you take very seriously?
